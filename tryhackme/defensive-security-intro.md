## Key Concepts

### Red Team vs Blue Team
- **Red Team** → Offensive security; simulates attacks to find weaknesses
- **Blue Team** → Defensive security; protects, detects, and responds to threats

### Blue Team Responsibilities
- **SOC (Security Operations Center):** 24/7 monitoring of systems and networks
- **Threat Intelligence:** collecting information to help the company better prepare against potential adversaries.
- **DFIR (Digital Forensics & Incident Response):** Investigating breaches and recovering evidence (the four stages and the importance of a forensic image)
- **Malware Analysis:** Reverse engineering malicious files to understand their behavior
-**Malware Analysis:** learned about how to identify potential malicious IP adresses using SIEM and reporting them (escalating ) the potential incident to the right superiors

## Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Log Data** | Timestamped records of events generated by systems, apps, and devices |
| **SIEM** | Aggregates and correlates logs across the environment to detect threats |
| **IDS** | Passive : monitors traffic and raises alerts |
| **IPS** | Active : monitors traffic and automatically blocks threats |
| **Breach** | Unauthorized access to systems, networks, or data |

##  Personal Takeaways

- Blue Team work is continuous and operational, unlike Red Team's project-based nature
- SIEM is the core of any SOC — it's where all log data converges
- IDS alerts without action; IPS acts automatically — both feed into the SIEM
- Dwell time is critical: the longer an attacker stays undetected, the worse the damage
